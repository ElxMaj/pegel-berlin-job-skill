# German employment terms — reading a Berlin offer

Reviewed: 2026-07-12. General information, not legal advice. Say that to the candidate.

Two offers with identical salary can be very different jobs. These are the terms that decide it.

## Probezeit (probation)

- Legally capped at **6 months.** A contract cannot set more.
- During it, either side can terminate on **2 weeks' notice**, and dismissal protection is weak.
- The candidate still keeps full rights to vacation accrual, sick pay and insurance throughout.

**What to flag:** a 6-month Probezeit is normal and not a red flag on its own. But if someone is
relocating internationally on a visa tied to the job, a 6-month window with 2-week notice is a
real risk they should price in consciously. Say so.

## Kündigungsfrist (notice period) — §622 BGB

- Statutory default after probation: **4 weeks, to the 15th or the end of a calendar month.**
- The notice the **employer** must give **increases with tenure.**
- Contracts often lengthen notice. Longer cuts both ways: more security, less mobility.

**What to flag:** an unusually long notice period (e.g. 3–6 months) locks the candidate in. That is
a real cost if they might want to move again. Point it out; do not judge it for them.

## Urlaub (vacation)

- Statutory **minimum 20 days** on a 5-day week.
- Most Berlin tech employers offer **24–30**.

**What to flag:** an offer at the 20-day statutory floor is below Berlin startup market norm. Worth
raising in negotiation.

## Betriebsrat (works council)

Larger companies may have one. It carries co-determination rights and can contest dismissals in
certain cases. Its presence generally means more procedural protection for the employee. Mostly
relevant at scale-ups and larger employers, rarely at an early-stage startup.

## The offer-check checklist

Run through this for any offer:

- [ ] Salary — and does it clear the Blue Card threshold if a visa depends on it?
- [ ] Probezeit length, and the visa risk during it if relocating
- [ ] Notice period, both directions
- [ ] Vacation days vs the 24–30 Berlin norm
- [ ] Is the salary the gross annual figure? (German offers quote **brutto**.)
- [ ] Any bonus/equity — and is it actually contractual or just discussed?

Never tell someone to accept or reject. Lay out the facts, name the risks, and let them decide.

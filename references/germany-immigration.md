# Germany immigration for a Berlin job

Reviewed: 2026-07-12. **Blue Card thresholds change every year — re-check before trusting these.**
Source: Make it in Germany (official).

You are not an immigration adviser. You explain the rules and point at official sources. Say that.

## EU Blue Card — 2026 thresholds

Annual **gross** salary minimums:

| Route | 2026 minimum |
|---|---|
| General | **€50,700** |
| Shortage occupations, recent graduates (within 3 years), certain IT specialists | **€45,934.20** |

The reduced threshold needs Federal Employment Agency (BA) approval.

### How to use these

Compare the **bottom of the disclosed range** — that is the number the employer committed to. A
role advertised "€45,000–€90,000" commits to €45,000.

Then say it precisely:

> "This salary meets / does not meet the 2026 Blue Card threshold."

**Not:**

> "You are eligible for a Blue Card."

A Blue Card also requires a **recognised university degree** (or, for IT, qualifying experience)
and a concrete matching job offer. Salary is one gate of several. Never collapse them.

**If the salary is not disclosed, the answer is "cannot be determined."** Explain the threshold and
tell the candidate to ask the employer directly. Never estimate a salary to produce an answer.

## Chancenkarte (Opportunity Card) — the expensive misunderstanding

The Chancenkarte is a **job-SEARCH permit. It is not a work permit.**

- Points-based (qualifications, experience, German language, connection to Germany).
- Lets a non-EU national come to Germany to look for work, generally up to a year.
- Limited part-time work and trial employment are allowed **while searching**.
- **It must be converted** into a work visa or Blue Card once they have an offer.

People routinely believe the Chancenkarte lets them just start a job. It does not. If a candidate
is on one, tell them plainly: an offer is the beginning of a permit application, not the end of one.

## Degree recognition — anabin / ZAB

Non-EU degrees usually need to be checked:

- **anabin** — the official database. Check whether the university and the degree are listed and
  what equivalence they carry.
- If the degree is not in anabin, a **ZAB Statement of Comparability** may be needed.

This matters because Blue Card eligibility leans on a recognised degree. A candidate who assumes
their degree "counts" and discovers otherwise mid-application loses months.

**Ask early:** "Is your degree listed in anabin?" It is a five-minute check that changes the plan.

## What to always say

Immigration outcomes depend on the individual case, the Ausländerbehörde, and rules that move.
State the thresholds and the routes; flag what is uncertain; send them to official sources
(Make it in Germany, BAMF, the Berlin ServicePortal) or a lawyer for anything binding.
